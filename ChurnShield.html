📉 Deep-Dive Churn Analysis

😠 Complaint Count vs Churn Rate

Higher complaints = drastically higher churn
📌 Interpretation: Churn is very low (~18%) for customers with 0–1 complaints, but spikes sharply to 54% at just 2 complaints. With 6 complaints, churn reaches 69.6%. This shows that even one unresolved complaint can more than triple the churn risk — making fast complaint resolution the top retention priority.

⭐ Satisfaction Score: Churned vs Retained

Churned customers score significantly lower
📌 Interpretation: Churned customers average 5.59 satisfaction while retained customers average 6.31. A score of 6 appears to be the retention threshold — customers below it are consistently more likely to leave. Improving satisfaction by even 1 point through better service can significantly reduce churn probability.

💳 EMI Delay vs Churn

Impact of payment delays
📌 Interpretation: EMI-delayed customers churn at a slightly higher rate (49.5%) than on-time payers (49%). While the gap is small, payment delays signal financial stress — making these customers worth proactive outreach before the stress escalates.

👤 Age Group vs Churn Rate

Younger customers churn slightly more
📌 Interpretation: The 21–30 age group has the highest churn at 51.7%, while 51–55 year-olds churn the least at 45.4%. Younger customers likely have higher expectations and more upgrade aspirations, making digital engagement and modern loyalty incentives essential for this demographic.

🎯 Risk Level vs Actual Churn

High risk = 66% actual churn rate
📌 Interpretation: The risk model is highly accurate — High Risk customers churn at 66.4% in reality. Low Risk customers show 0% churn, confirming the model cleanly separates safe and at-risk customers. This validates using the risk score as a targeting tool for retention campaigns.

💡 Key Insights & Strategic Actions

🔴 Critical Finding #1

Overall churn rate stands at 49.2% — nearly half of all 5,000 Tata Nano customers have churned. This is a systemic retention crisis requiring immediate multi-level intervention.

⚠️ Critical Finding #2

Customers with 4+ complaints have a 65–70% churn rate. Complaint resolution is the single most powerful lever to reduce churn across all segments.

🔵 Critical Finding #3

High-risk customers churn at 66.4% — and the risk model is highly accurate. These customers should receive priority retention treatment through ChurnShield's recommender.
